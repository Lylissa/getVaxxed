How to Prevent Coronavirus?

The virus spreads mostly through droplets that people send out when they talk, sneeze, or cough. These usually don’t stay in the air for long or go farther than 6 feet. But the coronavirus might also travel through tiny aerosol particles that can linger for up to 3 hours and travel farther away.

This is why wearing a facial covering can be important. It can keep you from giving it to others as well as helping you avoid breathing it in.

Symptoms of Coronavirus

COVID-19 affects different people in different ways. Most infected people will develop mild to moderate illness and recover without hospitalization.

High Fever

Fever is one of the main symptoms of sickness from coronavirus infection, along with cough and shortness of breath. These symptoms can appear two to 14 days after exposure to coronavirus

Cough

If you develop a dry cough, it’s very possible that you could simply be dealing with allergies, being inside all day (ahem, working from home), or other environmental factors.

Sore Troath

A sore throat is pain, scratchiness or irritation of the throat that often worsens when you swallow. The most common cause of a sore throat (pharyngitis) is a viral infection, such as a cold or the flu.

Headache

Headaches are a very common condition that most people will experience many times during their lives. The main symptom of a headache is a pain in your head or face.
